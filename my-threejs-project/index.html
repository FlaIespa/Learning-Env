Latin American Leadership Academy - Marcelo Peterlini

    May 1st, 2021
    To whom it may concern,
    It is an honor to write this letter to enthusiastically endorse Flavia Iespa. I have known her for about two years and since the first time I met her I was already impressed with her energy and enthusiasm. Our first contact was through a 30 min Skype chat and after listening to Flavia’s story I could understand how she was able to develop such grit and determination in an early stage in her life. Her family never had the best financial and emotional structure to support her in the best way and because of that she naturally developed a level of maturity well above average for her age. She grew up being concerned about finding ways to support her family financially and also emotionally, playing the psychologist role with her mom. Whenever Flavia sets a goal for herseIf there is nothing that can happen that will prevent her from achieving it, she does not wait for opportunities to come to her, she pursues it deliberately and makes it happen, and that is how I got the privilege to meet her.
    I have been working in education and youth development for 6 years and I am currently the Regional Director at Latin American Leadership Academy (LALA), an educational NGO with the mission to identify, select and develop a new generation of young ethical and socially innovative leaders around Latin America. Our work is focused on developing the most promising teenagers in Latin America that have demonstrated leadership potential and desire to positive impact their communities. We do that by organizing week long intensive leadership and social impact programs, which we call bootcamps, to provide youth with all the tools, abilities and support to tackle the problems they are most passionate about. Our selection criteria for the bootcamp we ran in August 2019 was very stringent: all applicants had to demonstrate a genuine commitment to creating social impact, and show outstanding servant leadership, entrepreneurial, and intellectual potential. Finally, they had to show mastery of English. In a way, we are trying to identify at an early stage the students with a passion and potential to become the next future leaders of Latin America. I was responsible for selecting Flavia to attend the bootcamp in Lima, Peru.
    After an intensely selective process, we selected 30 students from all over Latin America. Flavia stood out from the start: since she is a very charismatic, eloquent and outgoing person, she naturally assumed the role of a leader in most activities that were proposed during the Bootcamp, manifesting her strong leadership skills among the carefully selected group of young leaders. Part of the Bootcamp goal was to support students to identify an issue they care about in the world and start understanding that issue more deeply before deciding to develop an initiative that might contribute for the solution of it. Most students of the Bootcamp were very quick in deciding the issue they would like to explore, but Flavia decided to take a different route. In an environment where you gather the most promising young leaders in Latin America, is very common for students to pretend to have clarity about everything they are doing to prove to others that they were selected for a reason.
    Although,Flavia is no ordinary person and she wasn’t worried about creating an image of herself that fit the expected patterns of the group, she was looking for truth and to understand what really mattered to her, and that says a lot about her character. One that note, Flavia impressed me with her willingness to share her truth and vulnerability at all times during the program, not worrying about how that would make her be seen by others in the group. I remember having a conversation with her where she opened up to me saying that she was lost and confused about the things she wanted to pursue with her life, next steps to be taken after going back home and not knowing who she was. For me, that simple act was a demonstration of her courage; courage to be vulnerable and ask for help when everyone around her is telling her to be strong and deal with things by yourself. It fascinates me how Flavia is able to use her network to help her make sense of her life and, consequently, make the right decisions. She understands the value of the community and is able to use hers to achieve her goals.
    I was also able to follow Flavia’s steps after the Bootcamp and was very gratifying to see her spreading her light to other places around the world. After going back to Rio de Janeiro, Flavia started to develop a podcast where she wants to bring more voice to youth to talk about all the issues that are happening in the world. She also applied and was accepted at our LALA Academy Program, where we selected some of the most promising young leaders in Latin America to attend a 3-month immersive leadership and social innovation program in Medellin, Colombia. There I had the chance to spend even more time with Flavia and was surprised by the grit and growth mindset she demonstrated. Her eagerness to learn is off the chart. During my six years working with education, I have never seen a student taking ownership of his or her learning so beautifully like Flavia. During the 3 month program, she became very interested in understanding contemplative practices, Buddhism, and meditation. It was fascinating to see her commitment to growing in these three areas. Flavia started to attend Buddhist teachings sessions once a week at a nearby Buddhist Center, practiced one hour of meditation every single day in the morning prior to class, read three books focused in contemplative practices in less than two weeks, was constantly watching videos and reading articles about those topics and was looking for any opportunity she could find to refine her meditation practice.
    Today, Flavia is working with me as an intern, supporting the admissions process for the Bootcamps we organize. Once again, I am delighted by her professionalism and strive for excellence. Her main responsibility as an intern is to support me with the interview process of students who have applied for the Bootcamps. It stands out to me her deep care and compassion for the students. Before each interview, she carefully reviews every student’s application, reading, and memorizing each detail of it. During the interview, she is very skilled in holding a space where students can feel very comfortable sharing who they are. I can clearly see in her traits of a phenomenal compassionate leader, just by the way in which she offers attentive listening, presence and a liturgical follow up to student’s answers in response to her questions. After a few seasons in the admissions team, Flavia started to mentor new interns and, very quickly, became an inspiration and reference to them. It was very common to have interns sharing all the learnings they were receiving from her and the care she put into supporting them.
    Recently, Flávia has started to volunteer at the CVV (Centro da Valorização da Vida). Her main role as a volunteer is to receive calls from people who are going through depression and suicidal thoughts, and offer them care by deeply listening to their stories and providing words of comfort and support. When she first told me about that, my heart melted. This was the type of action that is a clear manifestation of two of the main values that I associate with her, love and compassion. She is one of the people I’ve worked with that can actually bring a radical transformation in society; not one that changes our outer world, but one that changes our inner lives through unconditional love, acceptance and understanding. There are very few people who are willing to have the courage to aim at unconditionally loving and understanding the world, and Flavia is one of the rare beings out there. It warms my heart to see Flavia getting closer to her truth and finding her own path into this world. After a long process of self-reflection, Flavia is gaining more clarity about her desire to pursue a career as a psychologist. I am certain that her kindness and compassionate heart will offer healing and clarity to any person that comes into her life.
    I am personally excited to follow Flavia’s journey, which will undoubtedly be transcendental for the communities she touches. I will personally ensure I stay in touch with her to support her in any way I can since she has the passion and potential to transform Latin America. I will be happy to expand on this letter through a phone call if it would help you make an informed decision. But in short, I reiterate that I endorse Flavia with the greatest enthusiasm and with no reservations.
Yours truly,.
